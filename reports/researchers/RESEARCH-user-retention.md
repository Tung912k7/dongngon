## Research Report: User Retention in Slow-Consumption Apps

### Executive Summary
Retention in mindfulness, journaling, and slow-reading apps differs profoundly from standard social media. Instead of infinite variable rewards (like slot machines), successful slow apps rely on "cumulative investment" and "aesthetic progression".

### Findings
#### Finding 1: Aesthetic Streaks (Visual Progress)
Apps like Forest or Sleep Town use a visual representation of a streak (growing a tree/building a town). When applied to literature, tracking consecutive active days by visualizing the growth of a Bamboo tree, a Lotus flower, or filling an inkwell creates a calming yet compelling reason to return.
- Source: behavioral design patterns in mindfulness apps.
- Confidence: High

#### Finding 2: Collaborative Anticipation
Since Đồng ngôn limits contributions to 1 character/line per day, users are naturally forced to wait. Notifying users when it is "their turn" or when a watched poem has advanced creates an asynchronous hook.
- Source: Play-by-mail games and asynchronous collaborative platforms.
- Confidence: High

### Recommendations
1. **Recommended Method**: "Mạch Văn" (The Scholar's Streak). A simple, visually elegant streak system that tracks consecutive days a user has either contributed to or created a work.
   - *Why*: It's computationally inexpensive, aligns perfectly with the 1-contribution-per-day rule, and rewards consistency over bingeing.
2. **Visual Implementation**: Display a small, elegant indicator on the user's Profile and the Navbar (e.g., "🔥 3 ngày" or a minimal ink-drop icon "💧 3").
